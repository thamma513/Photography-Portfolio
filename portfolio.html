Keeper's of the Coast

The coastal marsh of Terrebonne Parish is in a state of transition. Salt invaded the once fresh body of water, and over time, a fruitful farmland has turned into a graveyard of oak trees. However, there is still an abundance of life. Some of the most vibrant residents include a stunning herd of free roaming horses. The life still in existence is a striking display of survival in a time of great change. Witnessing such a transition has been a sacred experience for me. I feel honored to be able to explore the land and interact with its occupants. The tranquility of this dynamic coastal habitat has affected my understanding of time, loss, and our position in the universe.
